What is Addition?

Addition means combining two or more numbers together to get a total sum.

Example: 7 + 5 = ?

Step 1: Start with the first number: 7

Step 2: Count forward 5 more: 8, 9, 10, 11, 12

Step 3: The answer is 12!

üéØ 7 + 5 = 12

Subtraction means taking away one number from another to find the difference.

Example: 15 - 6 = ?

Step 1: Start with the first number: 15

Step 2: Count backward 6 steps: 14, 13, 12, 11, 10, 9

Step 3: The answer is 9!

üéØ 15 - 6 = 9

üß† Problem Solving

What is Mathematical Problem Solving?

Problem solving means reading a story or situation, understanding what math is needed, and finding the answer.

Example: Emma has 8 cookies. She eats 3 cookies. How many are left?

Step 1: Understand the story - Emma starts with 8 cookies

Step 2: Identify the action - She eats (takes away) 3 cookies

Step 3: Choose the operation - This is subtraction: 8 - 3

Step 4: Solve - 8 - 3 = 5

üéØ Emma has 5 cookies left!

üìê Linear Equations

What are Linear Equations?

Linear equations are equations where the highest power of any variable is 1. They form straight lines when graphed.

Solving Linear Equations: ax + b = c

Example: Solve 3x + 5 = 14

Step 1: Subtract 5 from both sides: 3x + 5 - 5 = 14 - 5

Step 2: Simplify: 3x = 9

Step 3: Divide both sides by 3: x = 3

üéØ Solution: x = 3

Linear Equations with Variables on Both Sides

Example: Solve 2x + 3 = x + 7

Step 1: Subtract x from both sides: 2x - x + 3 = x - x + 7

Step 2: Simplify: x + 3 = 7

Step 3: Subtract 3 from both sides: x = 4

üéØ Solution: x = 4

‚öñÔ∏è Systems of Equations

What are Systems of Equations?

Systems of equations are sets of two or more equations that share the same variables. We solve for values that satisfy all equations simultaneously.

Substitution Method

Example: x + y = 5 and x - y = 1

Step 1: From first equation: y = 5 - x

Step 2: Substitute into second: x - (5 - x) = 1

Step 3: Simplify: x - 5 + x = 1, so 2x = 6

Step 4: Solve: x = 3

Step 5: Find y: y = 5 - 3 = 2

üéØ Solution: x = 3, y = 2

Elimination Method

Example: 2x + 3y = 7 and x - 3y = 2

Step 1: Add the equations to eliminate y

Step 2: (2x + 3y) + (x - 3y) = 7 + 2

Step 3: Simplify: 3x = 9, so x = 3

Step 4: Substitute back: 3 - 3y = 2, so y = 1/3

üéØ Solution: x = 3, y = 1/3

    What are Mathematical Terms?

    Mathematical terminology consists of words and phrases that have specific meanings in mathematics.

    Basic Number Terms

    Number: A mathematical object used to count, measure, and label.

    Integer: Whole numbers including negatives (..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ...).

    Even Number: A number divisible by 2 (2, 4, 6, 8, ...).

    Odd Number: A number not divisible by 2 (1, 3, 5, 7, ...).

    Prime Number: A number greater than 1 with exactly two factors: 1 and itself.

    Operation Terms

    Addition (+): Combining two or more numbers to get their sum.

    Subtraction (-): Taking away one number from another to get the difference.

    Multiplication (√ó): Repeated addition; finding the product of numbers.

    Division (√∑): Splitting a number into equal parts; finding the quotient.

    Equation: A mathematical statement showing two expressions are equal.
